Problem 3: Low Autocorrelation Binary Sequences (LABS)

The Low Autocorrelation Binary Sequences (LABS) problem is a difficult
combinatorial optimization problem. Given a binary sequence
s = (s_1, ..., s_N), with s_i in {-1, +1}, define the aperiodic
autocorrelations and sidelobe energy:

    C_k(s) = sum_{i=1}^{N-k} s_i s_{i+k}
    E(s)   = sum_{k=1}^{N-1} C_k(s)^2

and the merit factor:

    F(s) = N^2 / (2 E(s)).

The goal is to find s that maximizes F, equivalently minimizes E.

LABS is harder than Max-Cut because the cost contains nonlocal 2-spin and
4-spin interactions after expanding the squared autocorrelations, and because
the energy landscape is glassy with exponentially many local minima.

Implement and benchmark at least two advanced quantum or hybrid strategies.
Suggested strategies include quartic-Hamiltonian QAOA, quadratization, VQE,
simulated quantum annealing, warm-starting / INTERP / RQAOA, classical optimizer
comparison, hybrid quantum-classical search, or another novel quantum/hybrid
strategy.

Benchmark target:

    N = 20
    E* = 26
    F* = 400 / 52 ~= 7.692
    r = F_best / F*
    goal: r >= 0.85 using N_eval <= 5000

For N = 20, this threshold effectively requires finding an optimal sequence
with E = 26, since the next energy level E = 34 has only r ~= 0.765.

Report requirements:

(a) Implement E(s) and F(s). Verify on the N = 11 Barker sequence
    +++---+--+-, whose autocorrelations give E = 5 and F = 12.10 under this
    convention. State which two or more strategies were chosen and how LABS is
    encoded into a Hamiltonian.

(b) Apply each strategy to N = 20. Report best sequence found, E_best, F_best,
    r, and N_eval.

(c) Compare strategies with random sampling under the same total shot budget
    and with a purely classical baseline such as simulated annealing or simple
    Tabu search. Plot convergence curves, cost vs. iteration, on the same axes.

(d) Discuss in 5-8 sentences which strategy works best, why LABS differs from
    Max-Cut, and which design choices matter most. Relate findings to Ref. [4]
    or Ref. [6].

Verification instances:

    N = 5:  E* = 2, F* = 6.25, example +++-+
    N = 11: E* = 5, F* = 12.10, Barker sequence +++---+--+-
    N = 13: E* = 6, F* ~= 14.083, Barker sequence

Quartic Hamiltonian:

    E(s) = sum_{k=1}^{N-1} sum_{i,j=1}^{N-k}
           s_i s_{i+k} s_j s_{j+k}

Under s_i -> Z_i:

    H_C^LABS = sum_{k=1}^{N-1} sum_{i,j=1}^{N-k}
               Z_i Z_{i+k} Z_j Z_{j+k}

Terms with i = j reduce to identity, overlapping pairs can reduce to 2-local
terms, and identity terms add N(N - 1) / 2 to E. This constant may be dropped
during optimization but must be restored when reporting E and F.
